4.1 Why the Balance Sheet Matters
A balance sheet answers three foundational questions:
1. What does the company own? (Assets)
Factories, land, brand, cash, investments, inventory, receivables.
2. What does it owe? (Liabilities)
Loans, payables, deferred liabilities, leases.
3. How much belongs to shareholders? (Equity)
Share capital + retained earnings.
This single statement shows:
	‚Ä¢	Financial strength
	‚Ä¢	Growth capacity
	‚Ä¢	Bankruptcy risk
	‚Ä¢	Capital efficiency
	‚Ä¢	Moat and scalability
"If the income statement is the movie, the balance sheet is the camera configuration behind that movie."

4.6 Working Capital: The Soul of the Balance Sheet
Working capital = Current assets ‚Äì Current liabilities.

	‚Ä¢	Positive WC: Good for manufacturing firms
	‚Ä¢	Negative WC: Excellent for FMCG, retail (customer funds the business)

	‚Ä¢	Inventory days
	‚Ä¢	Payable days
